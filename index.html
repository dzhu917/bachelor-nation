🤷Contestant Elimination🤷

Contestants are eliminated each week, as the Bachelor/Bachelorette develops deeper relationships. We've indicated elimination week through color here: the lighter the color of the dot, the earlier the contestant was eliminated. Winners, therefore, are represented by the darkest dot in each cluster.

🌹First impression roses as an indicator of success🌹

In the first week of each season, one contestant received a "First Impression Rose". We've compiled contestants across all Bachelor and Bachelorette seasons by whether they received a first impression rose.

Drag the slider to view the elimination status of contestants for each week of the show.

Out of all Bachelor and Bachelorette seasons, 26 first impression roses were given out. Of the contestants who received a first impression rose, 7 (27%) of them would go on to win the show. In contrast, only 26 out of the 860 (3%) other contestants that have been on the show ultimately won their season.

🤝Winners vs. Runner-ups🤝

Only one contestant each season can be crowned a winner and get engaged to the Bachelor(ette). The show depicts the choice between the final two contestants as heart-wrenching and nearly impossible because of the depth of both relationships. What makes and breaks this choice? Do the paths of winners and runner-ups really differ from each other?

In the graphic, the top dot represents the winner and the bottom dot represents the runner-up

*In Season 11, there was a tie; the Bachelor chose neither of the final 2 contestants!

🥀First Impressions Matter for the Bachelorette🥀

The winner or runner-up had received a first impression rose across 11 seasons of the show. Of these seasons, the first impression rose mattered most for the Bachelorette, where 5 out of 6 winners chosen had the first impression rose, as compared to the Bachelor where only 2 out of 5 winners had the first impression rose.


In other words, first-impression roses make a difference more so on the Bachelorette!

💞Winners Go On More Dates💞

Most winners and runner-ups go on similar numbers of dates, explaining why the choice between the final two contestants is so difficult. But they help! There are only five seasons (across all Bachelor and Bachelorette seasons) during which runner ups went on more dates than winners.


In other words, it seems like the number of dates matters. Winners tend to go on more dates!
